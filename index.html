Hyaluronic Acid:

A Complete Guide for Your Skin

An iconic moisturizer that attracts and retains water for deep hydration.
Ideal for dehydrated or dull skin.

Skin types: Dry, Combination, Sensitive Objective: hydration, elasticity Frequency: AM/PM
See products with Hyaluronic Acid
Quick Summary
  • What it does: Hydrates, fills & improves elasticity.
    Pairings: Niacinamide, ceramides, glycerin.
  • Best for: Dehydrated skin, fine lines.
    Avoid mixing with: – (generally safe).
  • When to use: AM and/or PM on slightly damp skin.
    Risk: Low
What is Hyaluronic acid?

A humectant polymer capable of retaining several times its weight in water. In cosmetics, it's used in different molecular weights to hydrate different layers of the skin.

Names and synonyms (INCI)

INCI: Hyaluronic Acid, Sodium Hyaluronate
Synonyms: HA
CAS: —

Who is it suitable for?
Skin Types
  • Dry: excellent for retaining water under occlusive creams.
  • Combination: moisturizes without oil; thin layers.
  • Sensitive: generally well tolerated.
Skin Concerns
  • Dehydration
  • Wrinkles
  • Elasticity
How to use it correctly
  • Concentration and format
    Whey 0.2–2%; combinations with glycerin/urea improve water retention.�
  • Frequency and timing (AM/PM)
    AM and PM after cleanser/toner, seal with cream.
  • Layers and order in the routine
    Apply to slightly damp skin, then apply cream and sunscreen (AM).
  • Application tips
    – Use 2–3 drops; do not exceed to avoid occlusive capacity without cream.
    – Combine with ceramides to reinforce barrier.
Compatibilities & combinations

It is powered by… Niacinamide, Panthenol, Ceramides.

Best to avoid combining with… No known relevant conflicts. Safe with acids/retinoids (helpful for alleviating dryness).

Side effects & safety

Generally safe. Rare irritation from the product's preservatives.

Patch test if you have extremely sensitive skin.
